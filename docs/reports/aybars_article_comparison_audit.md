# Aybars article comparison audit

## 1) Article source summarized

- Title: _Quantum Computing vs Blockchains: The Countdown Has Started_
- Author: Aybars Dorman
- Type: LinkedIn article used as macro context signal
- Scope observed: market framing, urgency narrative, cross-chain posture, cryptography readiness topics

## 2) Claims extracted

- Strong timeline framing and language implying immediate operational risk.
- References to sovereign payment systems and cross-chain readiness.
- References to protocol-specific claims (e.g., Bitcoin/Ethereum/Solana migration framing).
- Mention of cryptographic standards and key-technology options in relation to practical migration.
- Mention of specific protocol claims such as BIP-360/BIP-361 and EIP-8141.
- Claims about Algorand Falcon adoption.
- Repeated “attack has started” / “solve in minutes” formulations.
- References to Project Leap and Tourbillon implementation details.

## 3) Claims verified

- Macro institutional pressure and urgency framing can be treated as contextual background only.
- Cross-chain framing is useful at strategy level when marked explicitly as comparative context.
- Custody and governance sequencing are operationally relevant for institutional readiness.

## 4) Claims not verified

- BlackRock framework on CRQC and decentralized ledgers (no primary-source confirmation included).
- BIP-360 / BIP-361 details (requires Bitcoin BIPs repo verification).
- EIP-8141 / “Lean Ethereum 2026–2029” (requires Ethereum/EIP primary-source verification).
- Project Leap / Tourbillon details (requires BIS primary source before inclusion).
- Exact “quantum solves in minutes” timeline assertions without explicit assumption set.

## 5) Claims rejected

- “The attack has started” as a near-certain operational claim.
- Algorand NIST-approved Falcon-1024 in production as stated.
- Any inference that current public deployments are demonstrably breakable by deployed CRQC today.
